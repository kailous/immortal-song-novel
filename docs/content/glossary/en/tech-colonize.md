# Technological Colonization and Peaceful Evolution
> Merciful Unification Doctrine · A Strategy of Dimensional Advantage

## Core Philosophy: Charity at a Higher Technological Level
The protagonist understands that ancient warfare is fundamentally a zero-sum struggle over scarce resources. By importing a 2056-era technology package, he flips the game from “fight over grain” to “create new surplus together.”

## The “Technology for Peace” Strategy
**Three pillars of survival**:
- **High-yield crops**: Introduce potatoes, corn, and other calorie-dense crops through breeding or bracelet intervention to prevent famine in cold and arid regions.
- **Energy reform**: Spread coal briquettes and simple stoves to reduce winter deaths in the north.
- **Modern medicine**: Open basic antibiotics and epidemic prevention systems to non-Han populations and create a civilization-wide myth of “medicine without borders.”

**Temporary Autonomous Zones (TAZ)**: Some defeated regimes may temporarily retain local customs, but they must adopt Song-language education and the Song monetary system. Economic integration becomes the mechanism of real assimilation.

## Winning Over Enemy Elites
Not every enemy is a villain. For capable leaders who genuinely care about their people, the protagonist offers a model of **shared civilization**. He brings them into Song industrial cities, maternity wards, schools, and illuminated urban life, using superior living standards to break their attachment to old systems.

**The real target of war** is not the common population, but entrenched interests—old royal houses, priesthoods, and slave-owning elites—who would rather bury their people than surrender power. Song armies often arrive among the lower classes as liberators.

## Strategic Side Effects
- **Technology leakage**: Civilian technologies inevitably strengthen future competitors.
- **Internal opposition**: Hardliners inside Song believe the protagonist is too merciful toward “barbarians” and wastes precious resources. That tension becomes a recurring governance problem.
