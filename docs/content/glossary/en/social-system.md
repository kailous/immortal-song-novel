# A Millennium of Centralized Rule and Civilizational Leap
> Social Structure and Class Conflict · The Ultimate Utopia of an Immortal Autocrat

## Core Tone: Forced Growth Through a Thousand Years of Pain
The protagonist carries the absolute despair of an oncoming alien extermination threat. There is no time to let humanity evolve over centuries. Under his violence monopoly and immortal lifespan, the new Song state **skips the long transition through constitutional monarchy and liberal capitalism** and lurches directly into a war-machine form of hyper-centralized dictatorship plus extreme heavy-industry socialism.

**Why the system holds**: Historically, pure socialism and absolute centralization are hard to sustain together over long periods. But the protagonist is immortal. He personally functions as the incorruptible monument at the center of belief, allowing the Earth Federation to maintain a centralized heavy-industrial socialist order for ten full centuries without immediate collapse.

## Three Structural Class Conflicts
- **The last stand of old elites**: Former southern gentry, European church powers, and medieval lords repeatedly attempt restoration through alliances, assassination, and sabotage.
- **The backlash of comprador and capital classes**: Once global trade matures, a new class of ocean-spanning magnates pushes for free-market capitalism and parliamentarism in order to monopolize planetary wealth. The protagonist eventually has to destroy the very corporate titans who helped build the system.
- **Labor revolt in the military-industrial age**: After centuries of fleet-building and armaments production, the lower classes eventually approach a breaking point and erupt into desperate mass strikes.

## The Three Pillars of the “Fairness Machine”
- **A cold class balance**: When even his own children exploit privilege or commit corruption, the protagonist signs their death warrants and executes them in public. This extreme impartiality creates a near-religious belief in the fairness of Song law.
- **Education that destroys heredity**: No political privilege may be inherited. Whether the child of the highest official or the child of a newly freed serf, everyone must face the same mathematics, science, and military-industry examinations.
- **The ultimate antidote: AI and clean energy**: After a thousand years of concentrated development, the Federation breaks through into advanced AI and controlled fusion, finally reducing the civilization-wide compulsion to sell human labor merely to survive.
