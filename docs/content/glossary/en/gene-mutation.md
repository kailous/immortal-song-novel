# Genetic Mutation and the Limits of “Powers”
> Bloodline Inheritance Law · Hard-SF Boundaries for Extraordinary Traits

## Core Definition: Mutation, Not Miracle
Quantum reset leaves the protagonist’s reproductive genetics in an unusually active and unstable state. His descendants remain mortal, but some may develop **beneficial mutations** that enhance specific bodily or cognitive functions.

**Highest rule**: nothing may leave the carbon-based biological framework. Every trait must remain explainable through biology, neuroscience, or anatomy. No fire-casting, no electricity projection, no telekinesis.

## Confirmed Mutation Lines
- **Hyper-recall**: Extremely dense cortical circuitry and rapid hippocampal neurotransmitter conversion allow permanent retention of visual and auditory input. Cost: chronically elevated body temperature and severe dietary pickiness.
- **Motor mirroring**: An oversized mirror-neuron network in the motor cortex lets the user infer movement trajectories from sight and rapidly form muscle memory. It is still constrained by the user’s physical body.
- **Ultra-low metabolism**: A special valvular structure in the heart and greatly improved oxygen-carrying capacity allow up to thirty minutes of survival underwater or in low-oxygen conditions.
- **Parallel cognition**: A highly developed corpus callosum allows both hemispheres to process information independently and in parallel. This supports simultaneous strategic reasoning across multiple data streams. The young prince code-named “Ne-Zha” belongs to this line.
- **Dissociative regeneration**: Bone marrow and tissue reserve enormous numbers of potent stem cells, enabling explosive healing after injury. Cost: it burns through months of stored fat and nutrients.

## The Tragedy of Inheritance
As the bloodline continues, both the frequency and intensity of these mutations decay exponentially. A few centuries after the Song Federation is established, these gifted descendants will fade into legend.

Their gifts also isolate them. Minds that run too far ahead, or bodies marked by unusual traits, deepen the family’s long-running atmosphere of loneliness and sorrow.
