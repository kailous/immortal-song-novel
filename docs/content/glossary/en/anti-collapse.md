# Core Anti-Collapse Rules
> Anti-Cliché Guardrails · Worldbuilding Safety Rails

## Rule I: Non-Omnipotent Solitude
The protagonist is the only immortal in the story, and his physical body remains bound by hard spacetime limits. **There is no all-seeing mind, no magical teleportation.**

If he is farming in Jiangnan, he cannot instantly rescue someone in the northern steppe. During long campaigns, he repeatedly opens blood-soaked reports in the rear and learns that his most trusted marshal has been hacked apart, that the woman he loves has taken her own life in a coup, or that the crown prince he invested in has been lost at sea. Every expansion of the map adds another tombstone inside him.

## Rule II: Technology Always Spreads
**Technology will inevitably diffuse, leak, and be stolen.**

When Song firearms are lost on the battlefield, engineers from Jin, Western Xia, the Mongols, and eventually western powers will reverse-engineer the wreckage with near-maniacal intensity. If the protagonist stops innovating, his soldiers will be killed by yesterday’s invention.

The Royal Academy’s engineers and factory foremen are mortal. Faced with gold, seduction, or threats against their families, key researchers will not merely betray the state—they may carry away the entire industrial blueprint stack. That reality forces the protagonist to build a brutally cold counterintelligence apparatus and conduct repeated purges inside the military-industrial machine.

## Rule III: Unification Is a Flesh Grinder
**Territory is not recovered by drawing circles on a map.**

Even with an alien technological edge, the millions of Song soldiers who charge into battle are ordinary human beings. They still freeze in subzero steppe campaigns, still die in malarial jungles, and still bleed out for every mile. Every day, rank-and-file soldiers open the road to the new world with blood and ash.

## Rule IV: Revolution Is Built by Mortals
Every Song rebirth is not a solo run by the protagonist. It is a **mass revolution paid for in blood and tears** by the oppressed.

At the darkest moments, the force that truly turns the tide is not the immortal “god” at the center, but **ordinary people awakened by the ideal of a new world**.

A peasant recruit who has only just learned to write his own name may grin and hug an explosive charge under enemy cavalry so the artillery can withdraw. A covert operative being tortured in enemy territory may still refuse to reveal a repeating-rifle design because he believes his children deserve a life better than an animal’s.

**The divinity forged inside mortals by revolutionary conviction is the real emotional anthem of the series.**
